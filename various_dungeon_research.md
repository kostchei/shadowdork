The *Shadowdark RPG* currently has six official *Cursed Scroll* zines. Each one introduces a completely different regional setting (or "zone") packed with distinct thematic flavors, monsters, and classes.  
Here is a breakdown of the zones in all six zines, along with three thematic dungeon concepts for each.

## **Cursed Scroll 1: Diablerie\!**

**The Zone:** *The Gloaming* — A gloomwrought, demon-haunted forest and fetid swamp heavily inspired by occult folk horror, witches, and warlocks.

* **The Rot-Bramble Maze:** A thorny, mist-shrouded labyrinth constructed from living, corrupted vines. Players must navigate the shifting, razor-sharp hedges while being hunted by an eldritch warlock's patron.  
* **Sunken Keep of Mugdulblub:** A crumbling, half-submerged castle slowly sinking into a swamp. It serves as a faction-heavy dungeon crawl where players must navigate alliances between warring cults, strange frog-men, and aquatic beasts.  
* **The Willowman's Hollow:** A subterranean root-system cavern beneath a massive, ancient weeping willow. The roots are infested with low-level demons and the lost souls of those dragged into the dark.

## **Cursed Scroll 2: Red Sands**

**The Zone:** *The Djurum* — A harsh, shimmering desert wasteland featuring towering sand dunes, hidden oases, assassin-sorcerers, and warring efreet.

* **The Brass Ziggurat of the Efreet:** A scorching, trap-filled pyramid half-buried in shifting sands. Inside, players must brave illusionary puzzle rooms, fire traps, and bound fire elementals to steal an efreeti's hoard.  
* **Canyon of the Ras-Godai:** A vertical dungeon carved into the sheer red cliffs of a desert canyon. Delvers must navigate treacherous rope bridges and narrow ledges while surviving ambushes from shadow-stepping assassins.  
* **The Sunken Bazaar:** A once-thriving oasis market that collapsed into a massive sinkhole. It is now a lightless, sand-choked cavern system where mad merchant-ghouls fiercely protect their lost treasures.

## **Cursed Scroll 3: Midnight Sun**

**The Zone:** *Isles of Andrik* — A frostbitten, Medieval Scandinavian-inspired archipelago defined by longships, ice-covered peaks, the Midnight Sun, and the Long Dark.

* **The Rime-Caked Sea Caves:** Tidal caves that freeze solid during the long winter. They serve as a base for feral Sea Wolf raiders and hide a labyrinth of slippery ice bridges suspended over freezing, jagged depths.  
* **Tomb of the Frost Jarl:** A massive longship burial mound entirely encased in glacier ice. Players must use fire to melt their way through blockades while fighting off draugr (undead warriors) and avoiding collapsing ice tunnels.  
* **The Dverg Forges:** An abandoned underground dwarven smithy powered by volcanic vents amid the snow. It is now overrun with fire-trolls and deadly, still-active mechanical traps.

## **Cursed Scroll 4: River of Night**

**The Zone:** *The Tal-Yool Jungle & Black River* — A dense, sweltering jungle environment bisected by a mysterious river, featuring ruined temples, beast-men, and basilisk cults.

* **The Overgrown Basalt Ziggurat:** A crumbling temple dedicated to ancient serpent gods, completely choked by thick jungle vines. It features collapsed floors, poison dart traps, and a fanatical cult of basilisk worshippers.  
* **Cenote of the Drowned Star:** A massive, flooded sinkhole. Players must dive into the dark waters to explore submerged ruins filled with aquatic horrors, racing to find air pockets to survive.  
* **The Canopy Village:** A suspended, vertical dungeon consisting of rotting rope bridges and massive treehouses high above the jungle floor, forcing players to fight giant arachnids while balancing over a lethal drop.

## **Cursed Scroll 5: Dwellers in the Deep**

**The Zone:** *Morzomotha* — A vast, lightless labyrinth (Shadowdark's take on the Underdark) full of eternal night, underground seas, and cosmic horror.

* **The Librarians' Chasm:** A multi-tiered, lightless library suspended over a bottomless abyss. Insectoid scholars hoard eldritch texts here, and gravity begins to behave strangely the deeper the players descend.  
* **Fungal Grottos of the Nuln:** A claustrophobic, glowing cave system overtaken by parasitic mushrooms. The spores cause terrible hallucinations, and the fungus animates the corpses of previous explorers.  
* **The Subterranean Sea-Fort:** An ancient dwarven stronghold built on an island in a lightless underground sea, now occupied by aboleths, deep ones, and their mind-controlled thralls.

## **Cursed Scroll 6: City of Masks**

**The Zone:** *The City of Masks* — A sprawling, treacherous urban landscape divided into opulent wealthy districts, sprawling slums, canals, and dark alleys.

* **The Rooftop Scamper:** A high-altitude urban dungeon traversing the steeply pitched roofs, clocktowers, and crumbling gargoyles of the Silvertop district. Players must evade the city guard, rival duelists, and deadly falls to pull off a high-stakes heist.  
* **The Sunken Thieves' Guild (Sewers):** A sprawling network of flooded aqueducts, smuggler's coves, and illegal pit-fighting arenas beneath the Gutterwash district. Getting lost means drowning in the dark or running into the city's worst criminal underbelly.  
* **Temple of the Hidden Face:** An evil cult's lair disguised as an opulent noble estate. Players must attend a high-society masquerade ball to infiltrate the mansion, discovering hidden passageways and sacrificial chambers just behind the walls of the ballroom.
